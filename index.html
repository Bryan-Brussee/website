    About Me

    Though some have called journalism a dying field, I’m not worried about its future. This talk comes from people who don’t understand digital journalism’s potential for fast, innovative and engaging reporting. Where others see a dead end, I see a path forward. It’s clear to me that journalism isn’t dying. It’s evolving.

    To meet the demands of a web-first news environment, I’ve learned to do more than simply write and report a good story. I’ve gained a rich understanding of HTML and JavaScript by developing interactive data visualizations, web applications and webpages for clients ranging from our school newspaper’s award-winning website to Roger Cohen of The New York Times.

    As web editor for our school’s newspaper, the Indiana Daily Student (IDS), I am always seeking to push story forms beyond text and pictures. I code custom webpages for our best stories and frequently code and embed interactive data visualizations to support daily content. These visualizations range from Leaflet maps to interactive diagrams built using a combination of JavaScript’s JQuery and D3 libraries depending on what best complements a given story. I also cover news for our campus, region and arts desks and write a weekly pop-culture column for our Weekend publication.

    I can’t wait to start working for a web-first publication where I can use my reporting experience, data visualization expertise and knack for storytelling to tell ambitious, in-depth and exciting stories.
