    Science Forecast Production

    In my junior year of college, I led a group of graduate students to produce an episode of the campus science interview program, “Science Forecast.” In the initial stages of the project, we chose audio scientist Rob Potter as our guest and analyzed his work to find a compelling but viewer-friendly angle.

    Early on, I volunteered to work as a class coordinator; always organized and punctual, I thought myself a good fit for a managerial/production role. As the semester wore on, that role of coordinator quickly ballooned into that of de-facto producer.

    I kept Potter, our host and studio managers on the same page for each step of the project while also guiding our graphics and research teams. Once we started filming, I managed the studio and manned a camera. I was involved in almost every step of the process, and the project shows not only my versatility as a storyteller and content creator but also my effectiveness as a leader and producer.

      Bryan Brussee | 2017
