    Refugee movement through Manus and Nauru

    I recently had the opportunity to collaborate with a team of student journalists and coders to create a data visualization for an on-campus presentation given by The New York Times’ Roger Cohen. The topic of the talk was Australia's offshoring of illegal refugees to Manus Island and Nauru. Using JavaScript's D3 library, I coded an existing Sankey prototype into the above diagram illustrating the flow and demographics of refugees detained in Australia’s offshore processing centers.

    Data was gathered from Parliamentary reports and analyzed and processed in Excel.

    Working in collaboration with Mr. Cohen was an outstanding opportunity to explain my work and further my understanding of how to tell a story across words, pictures and data.

      Bryan Brussee | 2017
